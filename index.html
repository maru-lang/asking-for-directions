❓ How to Ask for Directions

💡

Always Start Politely!

In English-speaking cultures, jumping straight to the question can sound rude. Always begin with "Excuse me" or a similar polite phrase.

Simple & Direct (A1 Level)

Excuse me, where is the [place]?
Most basic question
How do I get to [place]?
Asking for route
Is there a [place] near here?
Searching nearby

🟡 More Polite & Natural (A2 Level)

Could you tell me where the [place] is?
Polite indirect question
Do you know how I can get to [place]?
Friendly inquiry
Can you help me find [place]?
Asking for assistance
What's the best way to reach [place]?
Asking for optimal route

🔵 Extra Polite / Advanced (B1+)

I'm a bit lost. Could you point me in the right direction?
Admitting you're lost politely
Would you mind telling me how to get to...?
Very polite request
I'm trying to find [place]. Any idea where it is?
Casual but polite

🆘 If You Don't Understand

Sorry, could you repeat that?
Asking for repetition
Could you speak more slowly, please?
Asking for slower speech
So, I turn left after the [landmark], right?
Confirming understanding
How far is it exactly?
Asking for specific distance

📝 Step-by-Step Directions:

  1. Start at the Hotel (blue square)
  2. Go straight along Main Street, past the Grand Mosque
  3. Continue on Avenue Mohammed until you reach the Central Bank
  4. Turn right at the bank onto Boulevard Hassan
  5. Go straight past the City Park and Pharmacy
  6. Arrive at the National Museum (yellow square) - it's on your left!

Total time: About 10 minutes walking • Distance: 800 meters

💬 Complete Sample Dialogue

Tourist: Excuse me, could you tell me how to get to the train station, please?
Local: Of course! Go straight along this road for about 200 meters. You'll see a traffic light. Turn left there.
Tourist: Okay, turn left at the traffic light...
Local: Yes, then continue for another 100 meters. The station will be on your right, next to the bank.
Tourist: Is it far?
Local: No, it's quite near – about a 5-minute walk. You can't miss it!
Tourist: Thank you so much for your help!
Local: You're welcome! Have a good trip!

⚠️ Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ Incorrect ✅ Correct 💡 Why
Go to left Turn left "Turn left" is the natural phrase
Where is at the bank? Where is the bank? No preposition after "where is"
It's in front the mosque It's in front OF the mosque Preposition "of" is required
Where is the station? (no greeting) Excuse me, where is the station? Always start politely in English
Can you tell me where is the bank? Can you tell me where the bank IS? Word order changes in indirect questions
Take left on the street Turn left INTO the street Use correct prepositions
🎯

Word Order in Polite Questions

Notice: "Where is the bank?" (direct) vs "Could you tell me where the bank is?" (indirect). The subject comes BEFORE the verb in polite questions!

🌍 Cultural & Politeness Tips

🇬🇧 English-Speaking Culture

  • Always say "Excuse me" before asking a stranger
  • Use "Could" instead of "Can" for extra politeness
  • Say "please" and "thank you" - these are expected
  • It's okay to ask for repetition: "Sorry, could you repeat that?"
  • Confirm understanding: "So, I turn left at the mosque, right?"
  • Smile and make eye contact - shows friendliness

🇲🇦 Moroccan Context

  • Medina streets: "Is it easy to find on foot?" (narrow streets can be confusing)
  • Tram/Bus: "Which stop should I get off at?"
  • Helping tourists: Use landmarks like "behind the main square"
  • Jemaa el-Fna: "Go straight until you see the big square"
  • Riad/Hotel: "It's just behind the fountain, next to the café"
  • Grand Poste: Common landmark in many cities
💬

Thanking People

Always end with gratitude! Use: "Thank you so much!", "I really appreciate your help!", or "Thanks for your time!" This leaves a positive impression.

🎲 Practice Activities

🔄 Activity 1: Polite Question Ladder

In pairs: Take a simple question like "Where is the bus stop?" and make it progressively more polite:

  1. "Excuse me, where is the bus stop?"
  2. "Could you tell me where the bus stop is?"
  3. "Sorry to bother you, but do you know how I can get to the bus stop?"

🗺️ Activity 2: Map Role-Play

In groups of 3:

  • Tourist: Asks for directions to a landmark
  • Local: Gives detailed directions using vocabulary from this lesson
  • Observer: Checks for polite phrases and correct grammar

Rotate roles every 5 minutes!

🎧 Activity 3: Listen & Draw

Individual then pairs:

  1. Teacher gives directions: "Start at the mosque. Go straight. Turn left at the pharmacy. The bank is on your right."
  2. Draw the route on paper
  3. Compare with a partner - did you end up in the same place?

💡 Activity 4: Problem-Solving

Discuss these scenarios:

  • "You asked for directions, but the person spoke very fast. What do you say?"
  • "The person says 'It's near,' but you don't know how near. What do you ask?"
  • "You think you're lost. How do you ask for help?"

Activity 5: Real-Life Challenge

Homework:

  1. Write down 2 places you visit often in your city
  2. Practice asking for directions to them in English (aloud, 3 times each)
  3. Record yourself and listen - are you using polite phrases?
